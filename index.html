What we believe in

Interface

It is amazing to see how we shaped the world to fit our needs, especially in our daily urban life. It works: trains are scheduled, garbage is being collected, taxes can be paid online.

But sometimes a service can be ill perceived because of user experience. We work with the UX always in mind, since the interface should be part of the whole design.

Solving the UX is a matter of keeping contact with the citizens or the customers and thus, small things can have a huge impact.

Data

Data helps us understand and predict behaviors.

We predict behaviors from the context and not from personal information. This is efficient and ensures privacy by design. We think infrastructures should be the first things to adapt themselves, before citizens.

Data, seen as facts, are also the best way to challenge conventional thinking and status quo.

But data is a tool.

Openness

We crave for transparency because we want to be trusted.

We use and produce open knowledge in general (open data, open source, open hardware etc).

All our civic or environmental projects are completely open because we are interested in impact more than in profit.

Attitude

We are passionate about learning things and taking the unknown path. We believe this is how innovation is driven. We try to innovate in all we do, not only code or design but also in organisation or in the way we hire.

Working as hackers, we prototype fast to prove our solution works, and only then we become engineers to cope with all the "impossibles".

It takes grit and boldness to innovate and we focus on impossible projects to prove they aren’t.

  • 6element

    6element is a project aiming at reducing waste arriving in recycling centers. It can be seen as an interface between citizens and recycling centers. We designed a sensor that monitors both customer flows and filling levels for every bin - in real time.

    Then a predictive model can tell you when you should go to which recycling center. The information is provided through a mobile app that crowdsources waste position in space and time so that reusers can take advantage of it.

  • Bordeaux 3d

    We wanted to make it easy to represent and interact with the city. We created a technology enbaling a precise model of a whole city accessible through the browser. Nothing to install, no loading time.

    Not only this gives a playground to visualize infrastructures like transports in real time, but it is also the best way for the city operator to communicate with the citizens and adapt the cityscape to their needs and desires.
